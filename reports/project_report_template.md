# Strategic Analysis Report: Hotel Booking Demand & Revenue Optimization

## 1. Executive Summary
This report provides a data-driven analysis of hotel booking performance, focusing on revenue leakage, guest behavior, and operational efficiency. Based on an analysis of 119,390 bookings, this report identifies key strategic opportunities to recover lost revenue and optimize occupancy through targeted deposit policies and loyalty management.

---

## 2. KPI Definitions
The following Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) are utilized throughout this report:

* **Total Special Request**: The sum of all auxiliary requests made by guests (e.g., extra bedding, specific floor).
* **Total Repeated Guest**: The count of bookings from guests who have stayed at the hotel previously.
* **ALOS (Average Length of Stay)**: The mean number of nights (weekdays + weekends) spent per booking.
* **Booking Change Rate**: The percentage of reservations modified after the initial confirmation.
* **ADR (Average Daily Rate)**: The average income earned per occupied room per day.
* **Revenue Realized**: Actual income kept (Successful stays + Non-refundable cancellations).
* **Revenue Loss**: Potential income lost due to refundable or no-deposit cancellations.

---

## 3. Dashboard 1: Executive Overview
**Objective:** High-level visibility into booking volume and cancellation trends.

### Key Findings
* **Total Bookings:** 119,390
* **Total Guests:** 234,988
* **Overall Cancellation Rate:** 37.04%
* **Peak Demand:** August (13,877) and July (12,661) are the highest volume months.
* **Segment Performance:** City Hotels face a significantly higher cancellation rate (41.7%) compared to Resort Hotels (27.8%).

### Strategic Decisions
* **Dynamic Overbooking:** Implement a 15% overbooking buffer for City Hotels during peak summer months to mitigate the 41.7% cancellation risk.
* **Market Concentration:** Portugal (PRT) is the primary market; diversify marketing spend toward the UK and France to reduce regional dependency.

---

## 4. Dashboard 2: Revenue & Financial Performance
**Focus:** Yield management and revenue recovery.

### Key Findings
* **Revenue Realized:** $29.60 Million
* **Revenue Loss:** $13.12 Million (Leakage)
* **ADR Analysis:** Canceled bookings have a higher average ADR ($104.96) than successful stays ($99.98), indicating that high-value bookings are more prone to cancellation.
* **Deposit Impact:** **Non-Refundable** deposits show a **99.36% Success Rate**, while "No Deposit" bookings carry the bulk of the cancellation volume.

### Strategic Decisions
* **The "Secure & Save" Policy:** Offer a 10% discount for guests who choose the Non-Refundable rate. This secures cash flow and eliminates the 37% cancellation risk on those rooms.
* **High-Value Protection:** For reservations with an ADR exceeding $120, require a credit card authorization or a 10% deposit 7 days prior to arrival to verify intent.
* **Direct Booking Incentives:** Move guests away from high-commission Online TAs by offering "Direct-Only" perks (e.g., free breakfast) to improve net margins.

---

## 5. Dashboard 3: Operations & Behavior
**Focus:** Guest loyalty and operational efficiency.

### Key Findings
* **Repeated Guest Loyalty:** Repeated guests have a remarkably low cancellation rate of **14.49%** (compared to 37.8% for first-time guests).
* **Room Match Status:** 12.5% of guests were assigned a different room type than reserved (Mismatch).
* **Lead Time:** The average lead time is 104 days, providing a large window for guest engagement.
* **Engagement:** Guests with more special requests correlate with higher ADR and lower cancellation rates.

### Strategic Decisions
* **Anchor Guest Program:** Prioritize availability for repeated guests during peak seasons. They are 2.5x more likely to complete their stay than new guests.
* **Monetize Mismatches:** Since 12.5% of rooms are reassigned, offer these "Upgrades" as paid add-ons 48 hours before arrival rather than giving them away for free at check-in.
* **Pre-Arrival Upselling:** Use the 104-day lead time to send automated emails at the 30-day and 7-day marks, offering "Premium Service Bundles" to guests who have already made special requests.

---

## 6. Strategic Roadmap & Summary

| Goal | Action Plan | Target Metric |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reduce Revenue Loss** | Convert 15% of "No Deposit" bookings to "Non-Refundable" via tiered discounts. | Revenue Realized Ratio |
| **Stabilize Occupancy** | Implement aggressive overbooking for City Hotels during July/August. | Occupancy Rate |
| **Increase Profitability** | Shift 5% of TA volume to Direct bookings through loyalty perks. | Channel Mix % |
| **Enhance Experience** | Formalize "Mismatch" rooms as complimentary upgrades for repeated guests. | Repeated Guest % |

---
**Conclusion:**
By shifting the focus from total volume to **booking quality** (Non-Refundable and Repeated Guests), the hotel can significantly reduce the $13.12M revenue leakage. Focusing on these high-intent segments will stabilize cash flow and maximize the efficiency of the peak summer season.
